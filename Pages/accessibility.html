Accessibility Statement

Last Updated: January 2025

1. Our Commitment to Accessibility

into3.ai is committed to ensuring digital accessibility for all users, including those with disabilities. We believe that education should be accessible to everyone, regardless of ability.

We are continuously working to improve the accessibility of our platform and align with international accessibility standards.

2. Accessibility Standards

We aim to conform to the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA. These guidelines explain how to make web content more accessible for people with disabilities.

3. Accessibility Features

3.1 Screen Reader Compatibility

  • Compatible with popular screen readers (JAWS, NVDA, VoiceOver)
  • Proper heading structure and semantic HTML
  • Alt text for all meaningful images and graphics
  • ARIA labels for interactive elements

3.2 Keyboard Navigation

  • Full keyboard accessibility without requiring a mouse
  • Logical tab order throughout the platform
  • Skip navigation links to bypass repetitive content
  • Visible focus indicators for all interactive elements

3.3 Visual Accessibility

  • High contrast mode option
  • Adjustable text size (up to 200% without loss of functionality)
  • Color is not used as the only means of conveying information
  • Sufficient color contrast ratios (4.5:1 for normal text, 3:1 for large text)

3.4 Content Accessibility

  • Clear and simple language
  • Video content includes closed captions
  • Audio descriptions available for educational videos
  • Transcripts provided for audio and video content
  • Animations can be paused or disabled

3.5 Assistive Technologies

  • Text-to-speech for reading educational content
  • Speech-to-text for AI tutor interactions
  • Adjustable playback speed for video content
  • Compatibility with assistive devices

4. Mobile Accessibility

Our mobile applications include:

  • Support for iOS VoiceOver and Android TalkBack
  • Large touch targets (minimum 44x44 pixels)
  • Responsive design that adapts to different screen sizes
  • Orientation support (portrait and landscape)

5. Language Support

  • Content available in multiple Indian languages
  • Language selection clearly indicated
  • Consistent terminology across languages

6. Known Limitations

Despite our best efforts, some areas of our platform may not yet be fully accessible:

  • Some third-party embedded content may have limited accessibility
  • Certain complex interactive elements are still being improved
  • Some older educational content is being updated for accessibility

We are actively working to address these limitations.

7. Accessibility Testing

We regularly test our platform through:

  • Automated accessibility testing tools
  • Manual testing with screen readers and keyboard navigation
  • User testing with people with disabilities
  • Third-party accessibility audits

8. How to Access Accessibility Features

To customize accessibility settings:

  • Go to Settings → Accessibility
  • Choose from options like high contrast, text size, reduced motion
  • Enable screen reader mode for optimized experience
  • Adjust subtitle and caption preferences

10. Alternative Formats

If you need content in an alternative format (large print, audio, braille, etc.), please contact us and we will provide it within a reasonable timeframe.

11. Training and Awareness

We provide regular training to our team on:

  • Accessibility best practices
  • Creating accessible content
  • Understanding diverse user needs
  • Using accessibility testing tools

12. Third-Party Content

While we strive to ensure accessibility across all content, some third-party integrations (payment gateways, embedded videos, etc.) may have their own accessibility features and limitations outside our direct control.

13. Ongoing Improvements

Accessibility is an ongoing commitment. We continuously:

  • Monitor new accessibility guidelines and standards
  • Update our platform based on user feedback
  • Test new features for accessibility before launch
  • Conduct regular accessibility audits

14. Compliance and Enforcement

This accessibility statement is in accordance with:

  • Web Content Accessibility Guidelines (WCAG) 2.1 Level AA
  • Section 508 of the Rehabilitation Act (US)
  • Rights of Persons with Disabilities Act, 2016 (India)
